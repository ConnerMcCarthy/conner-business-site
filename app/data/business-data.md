This section addresses common customer questions and provides general plumbing education. Answers are intended to be informative and may not replace an on-site evaluation.

## General Questions

### How much does plumbing repair cost?
Plumbing repair costs vary depending on the issue, location of the problem, materials required, and labor time. Minor repairs may be relatively inexpensive, while major repairs such as sewer line replacement can be more significant. An on-site evaluation is required for accurate pricing.

---

---

### How quickly can you schedule service?

---

### Are you licensed and insured?
Yes. We are a licensed plumbing contractor in North Carolina and carry general liability and workers’ compensation insurance.

---

## Water Heater Questions

### How long does a water heater last?
Traditional tank water heaters typically last 8–12 years. Tankless systems may last longer depending on maintenance and water quality.

---

### Why am I running out of hot water?
Possible causes include:
- Failing heating element
- Sediment buildup
- Undersized water heater
- Thermostat malfunction

An inspection is recommended to determine the exact cause.

---

### Should I repair or replace my water heater?
Replacement may be recommended if:
- The unit is over 10 years old
- Tank is leaking
- Repair costs approach replacement cost
- Energy efficiency is poor

---

## Drain & Sewer Questions

### Why do my drains keep clogging?
Recurring clogs may indicate:
- Grease buildup
- Tree root intrusion
- Improper pipe slope
- Damaged sewer line

A camera inspection may be recommended for recurring issues.

---

### Is hydro jetting safe for pipes?
Hydro jetting is generally safe for properly installed and structurally sound pipes. Older or fragile pipes may require inspection before high-pressure cleaning.

---

### What causes sewer odors in my home?
Sewer odors can be caused by:
- Dry P-traps
- Cracked vent pipes
- Sewer line damage
- Improper sealing around fixtures

A professional inspection can identify the source.

---

## Leak & Pressure Questions

### Why is my water bill suddenly higher?
A sudden increase in water usage may indicate:
- Hidden leaks
- Running toilets
- Irrigation leaks
- Slab leaks

Leak detection services can help locate the issue.

---

### What causes low water pressure?
Low pressure can result from:
- Mineral buildup
- Pipe corrosion
- Pressure regulator failure
- Municipal supply issues

Pressure testing can help determine the cause.

---

## Frozen & Burst Pipes

### What should I do if a pipe bursts?
1. Shut off the main water supply immediately.
2. Turn off electricity to affected areas if necessary.
3. Contact a licensed plumber.
4. Begin drying the area to prevent further damage.

Emergency services are available for burst pipes.

---

### How can I prevent frozen pipes?
Preventative steps include:
- Insulating exposed pipes
- Keeping indoor temperature consistent
- Allowing faucets to drip during extreme cold
- Sealing draft areas near pipes

---

## Gas Line Questions

### What should I do if I smell gas?
If you detect a strong gas odor:
- Leave the building immediately.
- Do not operate electrical switches.
- Contact emergency services.
- Contact a licensed professional once safe.

Gas leaks should always be treated as urgent.

---
